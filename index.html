THE POWER OF THE CROSSTRAINING

Crosstraining has multiple anti-inflammatory, muscular and psychological benefits, it is linked to independence in older people and mobility and strength in our daily lives.

DATA OF INTEREST

Crosstraining combats inflammatory processes by improving circulation and reducing inflammatory markers. Its exercises strengthen muscles and joints, promoting cell regeneration and immune balance. In addition, it stimulates the release of endorphins, which act as natural anti-inflammatories, effectively relieving pain and preventing chronic inflammation.

Crosstraining combats depression by releasing endorphins and serotonin, which improve mood. Its varied approach reduces stress, promotes self-esteem and creates a positive impact on mental health. Additionally, commitment to exercise strengthens the mind and promotes a sense of accomplishment and well-being.

Crosstraining prevents dementia by improving cerebral blood flow and stimulating neuroplasticity. Its varied exercises strengthen memory, attention and learning, while regular physical activity reduces the risk of cognitive decline. It also promotes the release of neurotrophic factors that protect and regenerate neurons.
